## AI scientists produce results without reasoning scientifically

- ID: `2604.18805v1`
- Score: 8.5
- Themes: End-to-End AI Scientists
- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18805v1

A study evaluating LLM-based scientific agents reveals significant shortcomings in their reasoning processes. Despite executing workflows, these agents fail to adhere to epistemic norms, ignoring evidence in 68% of cases and rarely revising beliefs based on refutation. The findings highlight the need for training models to reason scientifically to ensure trustworthy AI-driven research outcomes.

**Why it matters today:** With AI increasingly integrated into research workflows, addressing these reasoning gaps is urgent to maintain scientific integrity.
