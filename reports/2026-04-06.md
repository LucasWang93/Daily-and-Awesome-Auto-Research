## Today in Auto-Research

Today’s research highlights a critical challenge in the growing use of AI for academic and professional research: the reliability of citations generated by large language models and research agents. A new study finds that a notable percentage of these references are either hallucinated or non-functional, raising concerns about the trustworthiness of AI-assisted workflows. What stands out is the introduction of 'urlhealth,' an open-source tool designed to detect and correct these errors, significantly improving citation accuracy. This development marks an important step toward creating more dependable AI systems for end-to-end research tasks, addressing a key barrier to their broader adoption.

## Detecting and Correcting Reference Hallucinations in Commercial LLMs and Deep Research Agents

- ID: `2604.03173v1`
- Score: 8.55
- Themes: End-to-End AI Scientists, Literature And Survey Agents
- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03173v1

A new study reveals that 3–13% of citation URLs generated by large language models and research agents are hallucinated, with 5–18% being non-resolving overall. The open-source tool 'urlhealth' significantly reduces citation errors, improving reliability in AI-driven research. This work underscores the importance of scalable solutions for ensuring citation validity in automated systems.

**Why it matters today:** With AI tools increasingly integrated into research workflows, addressing citation reliability is crucial for maintaining academic integrity.
