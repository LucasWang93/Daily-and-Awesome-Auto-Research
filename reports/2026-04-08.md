## Deep Researcher Agent: An Autonomous Framework for 24/7 Deep Learning Experimentation with Zero-Cost Monitoring

- ID: `2604.05854v1`
- Score: 8.55
- Themes: Literature And Survey Agents
- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05854v1

Deep Researcher Agent is an open-source framework that automates deep learning experiments 24/7, covering hypothesis formation to result refinement. Its innovations include zero-cost monitoring, capped memory, and a minimal-toolset multi-agent design, enabling 500+ experiments in 30 days at just $0.08/day. This tool could revolutionize how researchers approach AI experimentation.

**Why it matters today:** This framework is especially relevant as AI research scales, offering cost-effective and efficient solutions for continuous experimentation.


## Learning to Retrieve from Agent Trajectories

- ID: `2604.04949`
- Score: 8.2
- Themes: Literature And Survey Agents, Research Infrastructure And Benchmarks

By leveraging signals like browsing actions and reasoning traces, LRAT enhances evidence recall and task success in agentic search systems. This approach addresses the growing need for retrieval models optimized for AI-driven workflows.

**Why it matters today:** With AI agents increasingly dominating search tasks, this research highlights a timely shift in how retrieval systems are trained.

## GBQA: A Game Benchmark for Evaluating LLMs as Quality Assurance Engineers

- ID: `2604.02648`
- Score: 7.55
- Themes: Autonomous Discovery
- Link: https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.02648

The GBQA benchmark tests large language models (LLMs) for their ability to autonomously detect bugs in game development. With 30 games and 124 human-verified bugs, it highlights the challenges of bug discovery in dynamic environments. Current LLMs, like Claude-4.6-Opus, detect less than half of the bugs, underscoring the need for advancements in autonomous software engineering.

**Why it matters today:** This research is timely as industries increasingly rely on AI for software development and quality assurance.
