## Top Papers Today

## Latent-Y: A Lab-Validated Autonomous Agent for De Novo Drug Design

- ID: `2603.29727v2`
- Score: 9.0
- Themes: Autonomous Discovery
- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.29727v2

Latent-Y, an AI agent for autonomous drug design, compresses weeks of antibody development into hours by integrating literature review, computational validation, and lab-ready candidate generation. Demonstrating a 67% success rate in lab-confirmed nanobody binders, it showcases the potential to revolutionize drug discovery workflows. The platform also extends to other biologics, broadening its therapeutic impact.

**Why it matters today:** With increasing demand for rapid therapeutic development, Latent-Y's capabilities are particularly relevant in addressing global health challenges today.


## MiroEval: Benchmarking Multimodal Deep Research Agents in Process and Outcome

- ID: `2603.28407`
- Score: 8.7
- Themes: End-to-End AI Scientists, Literature And Survey Agents
- Link: https://huggingface.co/papers/2603.28407

MiroEval sets a new standard for evaluating deep research systems by focusing on both process and outcome across text-only and multimodal tasks. Its innovative framework highlights the importance of process quality and reveals challenges in multimodal research. The MiroThinker series leads the pack, with MiroThinker-H1 achieving top performance. This benchmark is a step forward in diagnosing and improving next-gen AI research agents.

**Why it matters today:** With AI increasingly integrated into research workflows, tools like MiroEval are vital to ensure systems meet real-world demands and adapt to evolving complexities.


## BloClaw: An Omniscient, Multi-Modal Agentic Workspace for Next-Generation Scientific Discovery

- ID: `2604.00550v1`
- Score: 8.55
- Themes: End-to-End AI Scientists
- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00550v1

BloClaw is a next-gen operating system designed for AI-driven scientific discovery, tackling key challenges in serialization, visualization, and user interaction. With benchmarks in cheminformatics and protein folding, it offers a robust framework for computational research assistants. The open-source project is now available for exploration.

**Why it matters today:** As AI continues to transform scientific research, tools like BloClaw are vital for overcoming infrastructural bottlenecks and enabling seamless workflows.


## ORBIT: Scalable and Verifiable Data Generation for Search Agents on a Tight Budget

- ID: `2604.01195v1`
- Score: 7.55
- Themes: Literature And Survey Agents
- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01195v1

ORBIT introduces a scalable, cost-effective framework for generating reasoning-intensive datasets, featuring 20K queries across 15 domains. The Qwen3-4B model trained on ORBIT demonstrates strong performance among sub-4B LLMs, highlighting the utility of synthetic datasets for search agents. The framework, code, and datasets are open-sourced for public use.

**Why it matters today:** With the increasing reliance on AI for research and search tasks, ORBIT's open-source approach is a timely contribution to democratizing access to advanced tools.
